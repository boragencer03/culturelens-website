CultureLens: Cartoons, Family & Culture

Cartoons shape how children understand family, gender, and society. This project compares a Turkish cartoon (Niloya) and an American cartoon (Wylde Pak) to uncover hidden cultural messages in children’s media.

Project Information

Project Name: CultureLens – Family & Cultural Dynamics in Children’s Cartoons

General Topic: Media influence on children’s socialization

Narrowed Topic: Comparative analysis of Niloya (Turkish) and Wylde Pak (American) in shaping family stereotypes and cultural dynamics

Research Questions:

  • How do Turkish and American cartoons portray family structures?
  • What gender roles are reinforced in these shows?
  • How might children internalize these portrayals?

Method: We analyzed 10 episodes from each cartoon, coding scenes for family roles, gender dynamics, and cultural values.

Niloya vs. Wylde Pak — Comparison

Aspect Niloya (Turkish) Wylde Pak (American)
Family StructureTraditional, grandparents central, hierarchy.Blended, modern, shared authority.
Gender RolesMother cooks, father outdoors, boys active play.Shared parental roles, equal activity across genders.
Cultural ValuesCollectivism, respect for elders, tradition.Individualism, diversity, self-expression.
Education & LearningLearning from elders, oral tradition.Learning from peers, problem-solving together.
Social LessonsObedience, respect, community roles.Creativity, independence, teamwork.

Key Findings

Family Structure

Niloya highlights elders guiding children, while Wylde Pak portrays a more equal family model.

Gender Roles

Niloya shows traditional division of labor, while Wylde Pak promotes shared and flexible roles.

Cultural Values

Niloya reinforces collectivism and tradition, whereas Wylde Pak emphasizes diversity and individual growth.

Discussion – Why It Matters

Cartoons are not just entertainment; they are lessons about life. Niloya helps preserve Turkish traditions, but may limit gender role flexibility. Wylde Pak introduces alternative family dynamics, encouraging independence but possibly clashing with local values.

So what? Understanding these portrayals equips parents to guide children critically, helping them embrace positive lessons while questioning stereotypes.

Tips for Parents

  • Ask your child what they learned from a cartoon episode.
  • Encourage comparisons: “How is this family like ours? How is it different?”
  • Highlight positive values (teamwork, kindness) and question stereotypes.
  • Balance local cartoons with global ones to broaden perspectives.

Limitations & Future Work

  • Analysis limited to 10 episodes of each cartoon.
  • Only two shows were studied, reducing generalizability.
  • Future studies should include more shows (Peppa Pig, Rafadan Tayfa, Caillou).
  • Surveys with parents/teachers could validate children’s responses.
